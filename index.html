⚠ Behind Rocket Delivery

Behind Coupang's promise of next-morning delivery are warehouse workers completing overnight shifts under extreme conditions, and delivery riders pressured to fulfill orders within 30 minutes.

Riders are classified as independent contractors — meaning they receive no minimum wage protections, no overtime pay, and no workers' compensation. All physical and financial risk falls entirely on the individual worker, while profit flows to the platform.

In 2021, multiple worker deaths at Coupang facilities were reported and investigated by Korean media. That same year, Coupang went public on the New York Stock Exchange at a valuation of $60 billion.

"The company's rapid-delivery promise was built on a labor system that treated workers as disposable."
